2. Using the ATM

ğŸ¦ Cultural Context

ATMs in Japan are widely available at banks, convenience stores, and post offices. Many ATMs have English language options. Operating hours vary - some ATMs are available 24/7, while others have limited hours, especially on weekends and holidays. You'll need your cash card and PIN. Some ATMs charge fees, especially for withdrawals outside of your bank's network. Many ATMs have a daily withdrawal limit. It's common to check your balance, make deposits, and withdraw cash at ATMs. Some ATMs also allow you to pay bills and transfer money.
