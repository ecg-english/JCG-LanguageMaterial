1. Making a Reservation

ğŸ® Cultural Context

Karaoke is extremely popular in Japan and is a common social activity. Many karaoke places offer private rooms that you can rent by the hour. Some places require reservations, especially on weekends and holidays. Many karaoke chains have English-speaking staff and English song selections. It's common to order food and drinks while singing. Some places offer all-you-can-drink packages. Karaoke rooms typically have comfortable seating, microphones, and large screens for lyrics.

2. At the Karaoke

When you arrive at a karaoke place in Japan, you'll check in at the reception desk. The staff will show you to your private room. Rooms typically have comfortable seating, microphones, a remote control for the karaoke system, and a phone to order food and drinks. Many places have English instructions or staff who can help. It's common to remove your shoes before entering the room in some places. The room will have a timer showing how much time you have left.
